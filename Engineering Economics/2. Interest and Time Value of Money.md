### Basic Concepts
#### 1. Simple Interest
$I = P.n.i$

#### 2. Compound Interest / Single Cash Flow
$F = P(1+i)^n$

#### 3. Nominal(r) vs Effective IR (i)
$i_e = (1+\frac{r}{m})^m-1$
- r: Nominal IR
- m = Compounding period per year
- $i_e$ = effective annual IR

Converting to smaller i

$i_{lower} = (1+i)^\frac{1}{period} - 1$

#### 4. Continuous Compounding
$i = e^r - 1 $

### Payment Series
#### 1. Uniform Payment Series
$F = A [\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}]$
- F: Future Amount
- A = Annual Payments

#### 2. Linear Gradient Series
> G = 0 in year 1, increases by G each period

$F_G = G[\frac{(1+i)^n - 1 - in}{i^2}]$

> If base A exists: $F_{total} = F_{UPS} + F_{LGS}$

#### 3. Geometric Gradient Series
if `i = g`
then: 

  $F_g = nA(1+i)^{n-1}$

else:

  $F_g = A_1[\frac{(1+i)^n-(1+g)^n}{i-g}]$

#### 4. Present Worth (PW) <-> Future Worth (FW)
$F = P(1+i)^n$

#### 5. Begining of Year <-> End of year
$P_{end} = P_{beg} * (1+i)$

Multiplying by extra `(1+i)` because each payment earns one more period of interest
