# Noodles Production in Chaudhary Food Complex

## 1. Abstract
- This case study was conducted at Chaudhary Food Complex, a noodles manufacturing industry.
- Practical knowledge of industrial automation and production process was gained.
- Performance of industrial components was observed and improvements were suggested.

## 2. Organizational Background
- Chaudhary Food Complex is a food manufacturing company located in Nepal.
- It produces popular noodle brands such as WaiWai, Tenz, and Kwicks.
- The plant operates 16 hours per day.

## 3. Objectives of the Organization
- To produce high quality food products consistently.
- To maintain uniformity in taste and texture.
- To meet market demand using modern manufacturing practices.

## 4. Production Details
The factory produces the following noodle varieties:
- Brown noodles
- White noodles
- Snack noodles
- Bhujia and snacks

## 5. Case Study Objectives

- To acquire knowledge regarding the existing system of the industry.
- To give suggestions on how to improve the current status of the industry.

## 6. Existing System

**1. Mixing Unit**
- Required amount of raw materials such as flour, water, salt and other ingredients is measured manually.
- All ingredients are mixed together to prepare dough.
- Dough is then fed to the thickness control unit manually by workers.

**2. Thickness Control Unit**
- Dough is passed through a pair of rollers driven by motors.
- The gap between rollers controls the thickness of the dough sheet.
- Thickness adjustment is done manually by the operator.

**3. Shaping Unit**
- Dough sheet is passed through shaping rollers to form noodle strands.
- Shape and size of noodles is maintained by the roller design.

**4. Steaming Unit**
- Shaped noodles are passed through a steam chamber at 90–95°C to partially cook them.
- Temperature is set and monitored manually by the operator which may cause inconsistency.

**5. Cutting Unit**
- Steamed noodles are cut into required size using cutting blades.
- Weight of each noodle block is checked manually by workers.

**6. Flavouring Unit**
- Seasoning soup is sprayed or applied onto noodles to provide the required flavour.

**7. Frying Unit**
- Noodles are fried in palm oil maintained at 170–180°C to give crispy texture and increase shelf life.
- Temperature is monitored manually by the operator.

**8. Drying Unit**
- Fried noodles are passed through a drying chamber to remove excess oil and moisture.

**9. Packaging Unit**
- Dried noodles are packed using a digital packaging machine.
- Manufacturing date and other details are printed on the packet.

**10. Quality Control Unit**
- Final product is inspected manually by workers for weight, shape and appearance.
- Defective packets are removed by hand.

## 6. Limitations of Existing System

- Manual measurement of raw materials in mixing unit is time consuming and leads to inconsistency in dough quality.
- Thickness control using motors requires frequent maintenance and is not very precise.
- Temperature in steaming and frying units is controlled manually which can cause errors and inconsistent product quality.
- Weight of noodle blocks in cutting unit is checked manually which is slow and inaccurate.
- Quality control is done manually by workers which is time consuming and unreliable.
- High dependency on human labor increases chances of error and production downtime.
- No automated feedback system to detect and correct faults in real time.


## 7. Proposed System

## 8. Working Procedure

**1. Mixing Unit**
- Weight sensor measures the exact amount of flour and dry ingredients automatically.
- Level sensor detects the level of liquid ingredients such as water and oil.
- Ingredients are automatically fed in correct proportion, eliminating manual error.

**2. Thickness Control Unit**
- Width/proximity sensor continuously monitors the thickness of dough sheet.
- If thickness deviates from set value, motor speed is adjusted automatically via microcontroller.

**3. Shaping Unit**
- No major change, but sensors ensure consistent roller pressure and alignment.

**4. Steaming Unit**
- Temperature sensor (thermocouple) continuously monitors steam chamber temperature.
- Output of thermocouple is fed to ADC which converts analog signal to digital.
- Microcontroller processes the digital value and controls heater to maintain 90–95°C automatically.

**5. Cutting Unit**
- Weight sensor checks the weight of each noodle block after cutting.
- Underweight or overweight blocks are automatically rejected using a reject mechanism.

**6. Flavouring Unit**
- Flow sensor controls the amount of seasoning applied to ensure uniform flavouring.

**7. Frying Unit**
- Temperature sensor (thermocouple) monitors oil temperature continuously.
- Microcontroller maintains frying temperature at 170–180°C automatically.
- Prevents over-frying or under-frying of noodles.

**8. Drying Unit**
- Temperature and humidity sensor ensures proper drying conditions are maintained.

**9. Packaging Unit**
- Counter sensor counts the number of packets produced automatically.
- Printer automatically prints manufacturing date, expiry date and weight on each packet.

**10. Quality Control Unit**
- Weight sensor checks final packet weight automatically.
- Defective packets are detected and rejected automatically without manual inspection.


## 9. Benefits of Proposed System to the Company

- **Reduced labor cost** — Automation replaces manual work in mixing, quality control and packaging, reducing number of workers required.
- **Consistent product quality** — Sensors maintain precise temperature, weight and thickness at every stage, ensuring uniform quality in every batch.
- **Increased production speed** — Automated processes are faster than manual operations, allowing higher output in same working hours.
- **Reduced human error** — Microcontroller based control eliminates errors caused by manual monitoring and adjustment.
- **Real time fault detection** — Sensors detect faults immediately and corrective action is taken automatically, reducing wastage and downtime.
- **Better hygiene** — Less human contact with the product during production improves food hygiene and safety standards.
- **Long term cost saving** — Initial investment in automation is recovered over time through reduced labor, less wastage and higher efficiency.

## 10. Problems After Implementing Proposed System

- **High initial cost** — Installation of sensors, microcontrollers and automated components requires large initial investment.
- **Technical expertise required** — Workers need proper training to operate and maintain the new automated system.
- **Sensor calibration** — Sensors must be regularly calibrated to ensure accurate readings, requiring skilled maintenance staff.
- **System failure risk** — If microcontroller or any sensor fails, entire production line may stop causing downtime.
- **No manual backup** — In case of system failure, manual operation becomes difficult as workers are no longer trained for it.
- **Software/hardware compatibility** — Integrating new automated system with existing machinery may cause compatibility issues.

## 11. Recommendations

- Steam generated in the steaming unit should be stored and reused to save energy.
- Defective packets should be detected and rejected automatically using weight and vision sensors.
- Seasoning ingredient packets should be automatically added to noodle packets during packaging.
- A centralized monitoring system (HMI/display) should be installed to monitor all units from one place.
- Regular maintenance schedule should be followed for sensors and microcontrollers to avoid unexpected failure.

## 12. Conclusion

- Practical knowledge of industrial automation and embedded systems was gained from this case study.
- The existing manual system of Chaudhary Food Complex was studied and its limitations were identified.
- A microcontroller based proposed system with sensors was designed to overcome the limitations.
- The proposed system reduces human error, improves product quality and increases production efficiency.
- Implementation of the recommended system will minimize human effort and help the company achieve better manufacturing standards.
