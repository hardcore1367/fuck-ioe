## Machine Vision

Machine vision is the field of AI that enables computers to **interpret and understand visual information** from the real world — such as images and videos — and make decisions based on what they see, just like human eyes and brain work together.

### Steps Involved in Machine Vision

**1. Image Acquisition**
The first step is capturing the visual input using cameras, sensors, or scanners. The quality of the image directly affects all subsequent steps.
*Example: A camera on a production line capturing images of products.*

**2. Pre-processing**
The captured image is enhanced and cleaned — noise is removed, brightness and contrast are adjusted, and the image is resized or normalized to make further processing easier.
*Example: Removing blur and adjusting lighting from a dark factory image.*

**3. Segmentation**
The image is divided into meaningful regions — separating the object of interest from the background. This isolates the parts that need to be analyzed.
*Example: Separating a car from the road background in a traffic image.*

**4. Feature Extraction**
Important characteristics of the segmented object are extracted — such as edges, shapes, colors, textures, and corners. These features are what the system uses to identify and classify objects.
*Example: Extracting the shape and size of a fruit to determine its type.*

**5. Classification and Recognition**
The extracted features are matched against known patterns using machine learning or deep learning models to identify and classify the object.
*Example: Recognizing that the extracted features match a "defective product" pattern.*

**6. Decision Making and Output**
Based on the classification result, the system makes a decision and produces an output — triggering an action, raising an alert, or displaying a result.
*Example: Automatically rejecting a defective product on the assembly line.*

### Steps Summary

```

      ↓

      ↓

```

### Applications of Machine Vision

**1. Quality Control in Manufacturing**
Detects defects, cracks, or irregularities in products on assembly lines automatically without human inspection.
*Example: Checking bottles for cracks in a beverage factory.*

**2. Medical Image Analysis**
Analyzes X-rays, MRI scans, and CT scans to detect diseases and abnormalities with high accuracy.
*Example: Detecting tumors in brain MRI scans.*

**3. Facial Recognition**
Identifies or verifies a person's identity from their facial features.
*Example: Unlocking a phone using face ID.*

**4. Autonomous Vehicles**
Enables self-driving cars to detect roads, traffic signs, pedestrians, and obstacles in real time.
*Example: Tesla autopilot detecting lane markings and other vehicles.*

**5. Agricultural Monitoring**
Analyzes crop images to detect disease, measure growth, and assess soil conditions.
*Example: Drones scanning fields to identify infected plants.*

**6. Security and Surveillance**
Monitors camera feeds to detect suspicious activity, intruders, or unauthorized access automatically.
*Example: Airport security cameras detecting unattended baggage.*
