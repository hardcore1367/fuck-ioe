## 1. Mutually Exclusive, Independent & Contingent Projects

**Mutually Exclusive:** Selecting one eliminates all others. Only one can be chosen.
> Example: Build a road OR a railway on the same route.

**Independent:** Selection of one does not affect others. All viable ones can be selected.
> Example: Buy a machine AND renovate the office — both can be done.

**Contingent:** One project depends on another being selected first.
> Example: Software upgrade only if new hardware is purchased.

| Type | Select Multiple? | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Mutually Exclusive | ❌ No | Road vs Railway |
| Independent | ✅ Yes | Machine + Renovation |
| Contingent | ✅ If prerequisite met | Software needs Hardware |

## 2. Why Incremental Analysis is Needed / How Performed

**Why needed:** Choosing the highest IRR among mutually exclusive projects can be wrong. A smaller project may have higher IRR but create less wealth than a larger one.

**How performed:**
1. Rank alternatives by increasing initial investment
2. Take the smallest as **defender**, next as **challenger**
3. Calculate **incremental cash flows** (Challenger − Defender)
4. If **ΔIRR ≥ MARR** → challenger wins, else defender stays
5. Repeat until one remains → that is the best choice

## 3. Financial vs Economic Analysis

| Basis | Financial | Economic |
|---|---|---|
| Perspective | Private firm | Society/nation |
| Prices | Market prices | Shadow prices |
| Taxes | Included | Excluded |
| Externalities | Ignored | Included |
| Discount rate | MARR | Social discount rate |

> Financial asks *"Is it profitable for me?"* Economic asks *"Is it good for society?"*


## 4. Techniques for Comparing Unequal Useful Lives

**Method 1 — LCM Method:** Extend both projects to Least Common Multiple of their lives by repeating the shorter one. Compare NPW over this common period.
> A = 3 yrs, B = 4 yrs → compare over 12 years

**Method 2 — Annual Worth (AW) Method ⭐:** Convert each alternative to equivalent annual cost/benefit. Since AW is already annual, lives don't matter — compare directly.

> AW method is simpler and preferred. No need to find LCM.

## 5. Conditions to Apply Capitalized Worth (CW) Method

$$CW = \frac{A}{i}$$

Apply when:
- Project life is **infinite or very long** (dams, bridges, highways)
- Comparing alternatives with **very different lives** where LCM is impractical
- Dealing with **endowments or permanent funds** (scholarships, trust funds)
- **Public sector projects** assumed to last forever

> Concept: Deposit CW in bank at rate i — interest alone funds the project forever, principal untouched.

## 6. Inconsistent Ranking in IRR

**Problem:** IRR ranks projects by %, ignoring investment size. This gives wrong decisions for mutually exclusive projects.

**Example** (MARR = 10%):

| Project | Investment | Inflow | IRR | NPW |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | 1,000 | 1,500 | 50% | Rs. 364 |
| B | 10,000 | 13,000 | 30% | Rs. 1,818 |

- IRR says → Pick **A** (50% > 30%)
- NPW says → Pick **B** (1,818 > 364) ✅

**B is correct** because it creates more actual wealth.

**Solution:** Always use **incremental IRR** or **NPW** for mutually exclusive projects — never rank by IRR alone.
